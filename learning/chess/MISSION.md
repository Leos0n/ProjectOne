# Mission: Chess

## Why
Play chess online, climb a measurable rating, and enjoy the game as regular mental exercise. The goal is to go from "knows the rules" to a player with real strategy who can see their rating improve over time.

## Success looks like
- Has an active account on lichess or chess.com and plays games regularly
- Establishes a baseline rating, then improves it measurably (e.g. +200 points)
- Stops hanging pieces in one move (basic board vision)
- Can apply opening principles, spot common tactics (forks, pins, skewers), and win basic endgames (K+Q, K+R vs K)
- Finds games genuinely fun, not stressful

## Constraints
- 2–3 hours per week, in a few focused sessions
- Knows the rules but has no strategy yet — start from fundamentals, not openings theory
- Learning via this workspace plus online play; no formal coaching budget assumed

## Out of scope
- Over-the-board club/tournament play (for now)
- Deep opening theory and memorised lines
- Advanced endgame theory

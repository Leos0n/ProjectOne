# Chess Resources

## Knowledge

- [Lichess — Learn section](https://lichess.org/learn)
  Free, ad-free interactive basics: piece movement drills, board coordinates, checkmate patterns. Use for: refreshing rules and building board vision.
- [Lichess — Practice](https://lichess.org/practice)
  Guided interactive drills on checkmates, tactics motifs, and basic endgames. Use for: hands-on skill practice after each lesson.
- [Lichess — Puzzles](https://lichess.org/training)
  4M+ puzzles from real games, auto-rated to your level, filterable by motif (fork, pin, skewer…). Use for: daily tactics practice — the highest-value habit at this level.
- [Chess.com — Lessons guide](https://www.chess.com/lessons)
  Structured lesson track covering openings, strategy, tactics, endgames. Use for: alternative explanations when a concept doesn't click.
- [ChessGoals — Best free improvement resources](https://chessgoals.com/best-free-chess-improvement-resources/)
  Curated, rating-banded list of free study material. Use for: finding the next resource as rating climbs.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [r/chessbeginners](https://reddit.com/r/chessbeginners)
  Friendly subreddit for sub-1200 questions, game reviews, "why was this a blunder?" posts. Use for: getting human feedback on lost games.
- [Lichess teams/forums](https://lichess.org/forum)
  On-platform community; team tournaments are a low-pressure way to play humans. Use for: regular games and casual discussion.
